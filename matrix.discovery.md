# Discovery: MongoDB strategic architecture decision matrix

**Companion:** `matrix.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Thanks. This is built for **decision hygiene**: when stakeholders disagree, a matrix forces **criteria, weighting, and evidence**. I’ll use it to clarify trade-offs across **scale, ops model, cost, and team skills**—not to declare a winner by slogan. What decision are you trying to make in the next **30–60 days**?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

Decisions made by **loudest voice** or vendor affinity recycle old debates. Non-competitive is no explicit **criteria, weighting, or evidence**—so choices don’t stick and teams reopen them under stress.

## Cost of inaction

Cost of inaction is **re-litigation**, stalled migrations, and architecture churn. You pay for **duplicate POCs** and political damage when teams feel ignored. Execution slows because nobody documented **why** a decision was made.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. What options are genuinely on the table—not just MongoDB vs one competitor, but “do nothing” and build?
2. Who are the stakeholders with conflicting priorities, and what does each optimize for?
3. How will you weight criteria when trade-offs are painful—cost vs risk vs speed?
4. What evidence will you accept—benchmarks, references, security reviews, financial models?
5. Who has formal approval authority, and what format do they expect (memo, scorecard, POC)?
6. What past decisions went wrong because the evaluation framework was too shallow?
7. How does procurement participate—RFP, sole-source, competitive bid?
8. What timeline pressures could force a “good enough” choice—and is that acceptable?
9. How transparent do you want the matrix to be across teams—shared doc or executive-only?
10. What would a confident decision look like the day after you choose?
