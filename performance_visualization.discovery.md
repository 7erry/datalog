# Discovery: Performance graph builder

**Companion:** `performance_visualization.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Thanks—this tool is for **exploring performance visually** when numbers alone don’t persuade stakeholders. Discovery for me is: what story are you trying to tell—**capacity planning**, **before/after tuning**, or **vendor comparison**? Who’s the audience for the charts—**engineering leadership** or **finance**?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

Numbers without narrative fail in **exec rooms**—stakeholders don’t see why latency ties to revenue or cost. Non-competitive is spreadsheets nobody reads.

## Cost of inaction

Decisions default to **gut and vendor noise**; good engineering work doesn’t get funded. You repeat the same debates quarterly because **the story wasn’t visualized and archived**.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. What narrative are you building—defend current spend, justify migration, or prioritize tuning work?
2. Who skeptics do you need to convince, and what language resonates with them?
3. What metrics do executives trust—p99 latency, cost per transaction, incident count?
4. How do you source trustworthy baseline data from prod-like environments?
5. What internal approvals depend on a visual or slide-ready artifact?
6. How often do you revisit capacity plans—quarterly, annually, only after incidents?
7. What procurement or vendor conversations would these charts support?
8. Who owns data accuracy—platform team, app team, FinOps?
9. What would make a chart misleading in your culture, and how do you avoid that?
10. What follow-up do you want after the story lands—budget, headcount, or engineering sprint work?
