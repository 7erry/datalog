Thanks—this is an earlier take on the **Developer Data Platform** story; it’s useful if you want to see how the narrative evolved or compare messaging. ## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

Older narratives still shape decisions: teams **standardize on stories** instead of current capabilities, or dismiss consolidation as marketing. Non-competitive is **static mental models**—architecture reviews cite slides from two years ago.

## Cost of inaction

You mis-scope pilots (**missing features you didn’t know existed**) or over-build (**replicating platform value in-house**). Strategy meetings debate ghosts while **renewal clocks** tick. Education debt turns into **wrong RFPs and delayed migrations**.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. What has changed in your data strategy since you last looked at MongoDB’s platform story?
2. Which parts of the stack are you actively trying to consolidate, and which are intentionally separate?
3. What messaging resonates with your executives vs your engineers—and where do they disagree?
4. How do you align roadmap bets with vendor roadmaps when priorities shift?
5. What proof do internal stakeholders need before expanding footprint—cost model, risk assessment, reference architectures?
6. Who owns vendor relationships—central cloud team, data platform, or line-of-business IT?
7. What procurement vehicles do you prefer—direct, reseller, marketplace—and why?
8. What would trigger a fresh bake-off or architecture review this year?
9. How do you measure whether a platform bet is paying off—velocity, incidents, TCO?
10. What concerns do people raise quietly in hallway conversations that rarely make the RFP?
