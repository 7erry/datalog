# Discovery: MongoDB Atlas — compact DDP demo

**Companion:** `demo.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Appreciate you joining—this is a **tighter Atlas / DDP demo** meant to orient fast. I’ll hit the platform shape at a high level: what developers touch day-to-day, and what platform teams operate. Before I drive clicks: what would “success” look like for you after thirty minutes—**a technical proof**, **a procurement path**, or **alignment with your architects**?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

Short demos without discovery become **feature bingo**: impressive clicks, weak linkage to **your** constraints. Non-competitive selling skips **success criteria**, so the meeting ends without a shared next step or owner.

## Cost of inaction

You burn cycles repeating demos for **spectators who can’t buy**, while champions lack artifacts to **navigate security and procurement**. Deals stall not on tech but on **missing proof packaged for your process**.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. What hypothesis are you trying to validate in this session—technical fit, cost, or organizational alignment?
2. Who else needs to see a demo or recording before you can move forward?
3. What existing stack elements are non-negotiable in any future design?
4. How do you run POCs—sandbox account, production-like data, formal success criteria?
5. What security steps must happen before engineers can touch a trial environment?
6. What timeline are you working against, and what business driver sets it?
7. Who holds budget for Atlas if you decide to expand—now vs next fiscal year?
8. What objections do you anticipate from finance, security, or architecture—and what evidence would help?
9. How do you compare MongoDB to alternatives today—spreadsheets, trials, analyst reports?
10. What would you want in a follow-up artifact—architecture sketch, TCO draft, or security packet?
