**Companion:** `demo2.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Good to connect. This version leans into the **big picture**—Atlas as the place teams build modern apps with less integration tax. I’ll keep energy high but stay concrete: **data model, scaling model, and the services** you’d actually turn on. What’s your timeline—**this quarter pilot**, or **longer-range strategy**?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

Big-picture demos without grounding invite **“cool, but not us”** reactions—too abstract to map to workloads and stakeholders. Non-competitive is enthusiasm without **criteria, timeline, or sponsor**.

## Cost of inaction

Cost is **lost momentum**: execs nod, engineers shrug, and nothing hits the roadmap. Competitors anchor a **concrete POC** while you’re still aligning on vocabulary. Expansion revenue waits on **a narrative tied to measurable outcomes**.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. Is this conversation exploratory, or tied to a named initiative with a sponsor and date?
2. What outcomes would make your leadership say the meeting was worth the time?
3. Which teams would adopt first—greenfield products or migration of existing systems?
4. How do you socialize new platform choices across dozens of squads?
5. What internal blockers show up most—skills, standards, or funding?
6. What does your cloud and data governance process require before standardizing?
7. Who are the likely blockers or skeptics, and what would address their concerns?
8. How do you prefer to engage next—workshop, POC, executive briefing?
9. What commercial conversations need to align—enterprise agreement, startup credits, partner route?
10. What does “ready to pilot” look like on your side—people, data, environments?
