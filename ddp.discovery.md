# Discovery: MongoDB Atlas — Developer Data Platform

**Companion:** `ddp.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Good to meet. MongoDB’s pitch on the **Developer Data Platform** is simple in words and rich in implications: one place for **operational data, search, and increasingly AI-native workloads** without fragmenting your stack. I want to understand your **application portfolio**—what you’d unify first, and what has to stay specialized. What’s the one workload you’d love to **stop duct-taping**?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

The fragmented default is **best-of-breed sprawl**: OLTP here, search there, vectors elsewhere, glue everywhere. Each team optimizes locally; **nobody owns the integration tax**. Non-competitive means accepting **sync lag and dual security models** as “just how it is.”

## Cost of inaction

Cost of inaction is **velocity cliffs**: every new feature crosses three teams and three on-call rotations. Incidents become **multi-system mysteries**; FinOps can’t allocate spend cleanly. You compete against orgs that ship **one data plane** with coherent observability and access policy.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. Which data capabilities are fragmented today—OLTP, search, cache, vectors, analytics—and what does that cost you?
2. What integrations or sync jobs break most often, and who gets paged when they do?
3. How do you decide build vs buy for data infrastructure at your org?
4. Who funds Atlas expansion—central IT, business units, or project budgets?
5. What are your non-negotiables for a “default” data platform—regions, encryption, SSO, SLAs?
6. What competitive or legacy systems are still entrenched, and why?
7. What does your technical evaluation process look like—POC length, success metrics, security review?
8. Who influences the decision beyond the engineering lead—architecture council, procurement, FinOps?
9. What renewal or contract milestones could accelerate or delay a commitment?
10. What would “we simplified the stack” mean in measurable terms twelve months out?
