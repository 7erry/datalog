# Discovery: Changelog — MongoDB Unpacked

**Companion:** `changelog.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Quick intro: this deck is the **what changed** view for the MongoDB Unpacked material—useful when your team wants to know **what’s new, what moved, and what to revisit**. On a discovery call, my goal is to understand **how you consume updates**—do you need executive summaries, deep dives for architects, or enablement for builders? What would make this kind of changelog **actually useful** in your workflow?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

When enablement content is a **static PDF graveyard**, teams work from outdated narratives and repeat the same objections in deals. Non-competitive is **no single source of truth** for what changed, why it matters, and who should read it—so customer-facing folks improvise.

## Cost of inaction

Deals slow over **fear of being wrong**; solution engineers rebuild collateral instead of selling. New hires ramp on **myths**. You absorb hidden cost in **reworked RFP answers, repeated workshops, and lost expansion** because buyers don’t trust your story is current.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. Who consumes technical enablement at your org—SEs, CSMs, partners, customers—and how do they find it today?
2. What’s painful about staying current with product changes—volume, format, or trust in accuracy?
3. How do you distribute updates internally—wiki, email, enablement sessions, LMS?
4. What would “good” look like for a changelog—frequency, depth, or linkage to assets?
5. Do you need this aligned to release trains, SKUs, or industry verticals?
6. Who approves customer-facing or partner-facing technical narratives?
7. Are there buying or renewal moments where up-to-date collateral directly affects revenue?
8. How do you measure whether enablement content is working—usage, certifications, win rates?
9. What gaps do sellers report when they’re late in a competitive cycle?
10. If you could automate one part of keeping teams current, what would it be?
