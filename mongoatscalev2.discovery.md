# Discovery: MongoDB Atlas at scale — command center (v2)

**Companion:** `mongoatscalev2.html`

## Opening minute (discovery call)

Thanks—this is the **command-center** view of operating Atlas at scale: more operational, more “pilot’s dashboard” than essay. I’ll orient you to how teams **monitor, prioritize, and escalate**. Who owns MongoDB day-to-day—**a central platform team**, or **distributed squads**?

## Why current approaches fall short (without a competitive move)

Command-center thinking fails if signals don’t drive **weekly prioritization**—dashboards become wallpaper. Non-competitive ops confuses **visibility with control**.

## Cost of inaction

You still miss **cross-team hotspots**; executives get noise without decisions. Cost shows up as **repeated outages** and late capacity work. Platform teams drown in **tickets that should be patterns**.

## Ten open-ended questions (pain points & buying process)

1. What operational rituals do you run today—daily standups on metrics, weekly reviews, monthly cost triage?
2. Where does signal get lost between Atlas metrics, APM, and business KPIs?
3. How do you decide what gets fixed this sprint vs next quarter?
4. Who gets escalations when customer-facing latency degrades—L1, platform, vendor TAM?
5. What tooling budget exists for observability, automation, or FinOps around Atlas?
6. How do you align multiple product teams that share the same clusters?
7. What executive reporting do you produce, and what questions keep coming back?
8. What procurement path would you use for premium support or consulting tied to scale work?
9. What incidents would make you reconsider topology or vendor posture?
10. What would a “single pane of glass” need to show for your leadership to trust it?
